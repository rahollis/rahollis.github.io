Learning Goals:

  • Construct a system of linear equations from an application.

  • Represent a system of linear equations in matrix form.

  • Solve a system of equations with and without the aid of technology.

  • Determine if a given set of vectors is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

  • Find a basis for a given subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and determine its dimension.

  • Convert a vector from one coordinate basis to another.

  • Find a matrix representation of a linear transformation between \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(\mathbb{R}^m\).

  • Add, multiply, and transpose matrices.

  • Determine if a matrix is invertible and compute its inverse if it is.

  • Identify if a set of vectors is orthogonal.

  • Identify if a matrix is orthogonal.

  • Construct an orthonormal basis for a given subspace.

  • Compute the orthonormal projection of a vector onto a subspace.

  • Calculate the area of a parallelogram using determinant.

  • Understand the connection between determinant and the geometry of linear transformations.

  • Calculate the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix.

  • Apply eigenvalues/vectors to solve systems of LODEs.

  • Solve the least squares problem.

  • Compute the singular value decomposition.

  • Understand how to use computer software to aid in all of the above.

UBx uses Python to automatically generate and grade questions. In addition that are some problems that will require you to work in Jupyter notebook, so you will need to download and install the latest version of Anaconda. Don‚Äôt worry, any programming you will need to do will be thoroughly explained in the problem.

In-class activities: In addition to weekly homework, I plan on posting a Jupyter notebook activity here every week. These activities will be done during class or recitation and will not be graded.

Recitation participation: It couldn‚Äôt be easier to get 10% of the final grade, just show up and engage. Participation will be evaluated by the TA on a weekly basis.

Proficiency exams: Exams in this class will be handled differently than what you may be used to. Instead of having one or two midterms covering multiple sections of material, we will have short, 30 minute exams every other Thursday at the beginning of class covering only the material from the previous 4 classes.

Each exam will have 4 questions worth 4 points each for a total of 16 points. If your score is 10 or more, you pass the exam and will receive full credit. If your score is less than 10, you do not pass and will need to retake the exam. You can retake each exam up to 2 additional times. Exam retakes will take place during the first 30 minutes of class on Tuesdays. If you do not need to retake the exam, you do not need to show up to the first 30 minutes of class on that Tuesday.

Your aggregate score for the proficiency exams portion of the final grade will be the average of the individual exam grades (4.0 for passing, 0.0 for failing). There will be a total of seven proficiency exams, so to get a C, you must pass at least four of the seven exams.

Final exam: If you did not pass at least 4 proficiency exams, you will be required to take the final exam. If you passed at least 4 of the proficiency exams, you may opt out of the final exam.

For those of you that end up taking the final exam, I will reduce the weight of the proficiency exams from 70% of the final grade to 50%, with the final exam making up the remaining 20%. The final exam will take place on Tuesday May 16th at 8:00 AM, and you will be given two hours to complete 8 questions.
