**Learning Goals:**

    * Construct a system of linear equations from an application.
    * Represent a system of linear equations in matrix form.
    * Solve a system of equations with and without the aid of technology.
    * Determine if a given set of vectors is a subspace of :math:`\mathbb{R}^n`.
    * Find a basis for a given subspace of :math:`\mathbb{R}^n` and determine its dimension.
    * Convert a vector from one coordinate basis to another.
    * Find a matrix representation of a linear transformation between :math:`\mathbb{R}^n` and :math:`\mathbb{R}^m`.
    * Add, multiply, and transpose matrices.
    * Determine if a matrix is invertible and compute its inverse if it is.
    * Identify if a set of vectors is orthogonal.
    * Identify if a matrix is orthogonal.
    * Construct an orthonormal basis for a given subspace.
    * Compute the orthonormal projection of a vector onto a subspace.
    * Calculate the area of a parallelogram using determinant.
    * Understand the connection between determinant and the geometry of linear transformations.
    * Calculate the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix.
    * Apply eigenvalues/vectors to solve systems of LODEs.
    * Solve the least squares problem.
    * Compute the singular value decomposition.
    * Understand how to use computer software to aid in all of the above.

*Proficiency exams:* Exams in this class will be handled differently than what you may be used to.  
Instead of having one or two midterms covering multiple sections of material, we will have short, 30 minute exams **every other Tuesday at the beginning of class** covering only the material from the previous 2 weeks.

Each exam will have 4 questions worth 4 points each for a total of 16 points.  If your score is 13 or more, you pass the exam and will receive full credit.  If your score is 11 or 12, it will be considered a marginal pass and you will receive half credit.
If your score is 10 or less, you do not pass the exam.  If you are unhappy with your score on the exam, you will have the opportunity to retake it.  *If you opt to retake an exam, only your retake grade will be counted.*  You must have made an honest attempt the first time to be permitted to retake an exam. 
**Exam retakes will take place during the first 30 minutes of class on the Tuesday following the first attempt.**
If you do not need to retake the exam, you do not need to show up to the first 30 minutes of class on that Tuesday.  **We will still cover new material after exams and make-ups.**

Your aggregate score for the proficiency exams portion of the final grade will be the average of the individual exam grades (100 for passing, 70 for a marginal pass, 0 for failing).
There will be a total of six proficiency exams.

*Final exam:* **If you fail 2 or more proficiency exams**, you will be required to take the final exam.
If you failed at most 1 proficiency exam, you may opt out of the final exam.

For those of you that end up taking the final exam, I will reduce the weight of the proficiency exams from 65% of the final grade to 45%, with the final exam making up the remaining 20%.
The final exam will take place on Thursday Dec 14th at 11:45 AM, and you will be given two hours to complete 8 questions.
